Nissan Skyline GTR

Stock 99 Nissan Skyline GTR(R34)

The first generation Skyline (KPGC10, also refered to as just the C10) debuted in 1969 after the companies Nissan and Prince Merged. The Skyline came into to prodcution,
succeding the Prince Sedan. The sucess came within the Skyline's GTR model which came with sporty design, such as wings, and delivered with better driverain and motor upgrades
The Skyline GTR came in a longeivtal inline-six, Rear Wheel Drive layout until the arrival of the R32. The R32 came upgraded with the rally favored All wheel Drive format and
upgraded 2.6L RB26DETT (twin-turbo) inline-six. The R33 came with All Wheel Drive specs and the 2.6 L RB26DETT twin-turbo but also came with 2.8 L RB-X GT2 twin-turbo
inline-six option. The R34 came with the 2.6L inline-six or could be uprgarded to a 2.8L from Z-Tune. It also upgraeded from the 5 speed manual layout to a 6 speed. The R34 was
discontinued in 2002. The Skyline GTR was a foundational car in the JDM era and was feautured in The Fast and The Furious as a 1995 R33 and also became one of Paul Walker's
signature cars as it's apperance in 2 Fast 2 Furious as a 1999 R34. Many Skylines had to be imported from Japan and be tamed by American laws. It had to be properly inspected
and fit to the regulations. After the annoucned success of the legendary car, Nissan launched the GTR, which dropped the Skyline name, in 2007. This model came with a 3.8L VR38DETT V6
and showcased a dual clutch transmission. This transmission runs as a semi-automatic, meaning it can be shifted through paddle shifters mounted on the wheel.
